About

Hello! I'm Jeremy, a sophomore at the University of Notre Dame studying Computer Science. I'm also on the Men's Swim Team specializing in sprint freestyle and IM, and I'm a resident of Carroll Hall (AAMW).


I grew up in Georgetown, Texas with two older brothers and three dogs. I started swimming when I was four years old, and it's been a central part of my life ever since. I attended Georgetown High School and swam for Nitro Swimming.


I initially attended the University of Texas at Austin. During my first year at UT, I studied aerospace engineering while competing on the swim team. Our team won both the SEC Championship and the NCAA National Championship that season. Later, changes in the NCAA landscape led me to transfer to Notre Dame, where I shifted my academic focus to computer science. Best decision ever.


I'm very interested in machine learning, and I want to get under the hood to understand the mechanics, math, and systems behind the magic. Teaching and mentoring are also passions of mine. I love learning and building things, and I want to help other people do the same. In my opinion, seeing someone succeed because of guidance or support you gave them is one of the most fulfilling experiences.


In terms of work experience, I am a cofounder of the nonprofit organization Ten at a Time, which I remained involved with for over ten years. I also worked as a cave tour guide.


Outside of school, I spend most of my time with friends and family, exploring nature, hiking, and traveling whenever I get the chance. I'm also a huge fan of all things movies, music, anime, and sci-fi / fantasy books. I also love good food (especially good bread), but who doesn't.

Projects

Brain Tumor MRI Classifier complete

My team, Marco Basile, Tyler Asmussen, and Oden Ineza, and I built a machine-learning pipeline that trains and evaluates four models to classify brain MRI slices as glioma, meningioma, pituitary tumor, or no tumor. The MobileNetV2 model performed best at 91.4% validation accuracy. Additionally, we built a Streamlit dashboard where you can upload an MRI slice and compare all four models' predictions side by side.

Tetris complete

Built a Tetris clone in C using the X11 gfx library, representing the board as a grid and rendering it directly onto the graphics window. Block rotation is done via a 90-degree matrix transpose, with collision and edge detection to stop rotations into occupied cells or off the board. Since gfx2's rectangle rendering was unreliable, I wrote a custom proxy for gfx_fill_rectangle built from layered circles, giving the pieces their own distinct look. Playable with keyboard controls for movement, rotation, and quitting; the game ends when a piece stacks to the top row.

Alzheimer's Early Detection Platform complete

A two-stage, caregiver-led digital solution proposed for the Lilly Student-Athlete Micro Project, aimed at closing the ~3.5-year gap between when families notice early Alzheimer's symptoms and diagnosis. Stage one is a non-diagnostic screening survey that turns caregiver observations into explainable risk bands using symptom-frequency patterns rather than a black-box model. Stage two is offered for moderate/high-risk cases, letting caregivers log changes over time and generating structured summaries for primary care visits. We designed the system architecture and produced the pitch script, mockups, and slide deck. Won the 2026 Lilly Student-Athlete Micro Project MVP and Game Changer awards.
